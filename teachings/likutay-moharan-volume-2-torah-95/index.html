Likutay Moharan Volume II - Torah 95

"נֵר שֶׁל מִצְווֹת מִנֵּר שֶׁל מִצְוָה קוֹדֶמֶת"

The candle of mitzvot [commandments] is lit from the candle of the previous mitzvah. For one mitzvah drags another mitzvah, as our Sages, of blessed memory, said (Avos 4:2): "A mitzvah drags another mitzvah." And this is similar to lighting a candle from another candle. When one candle is lit from another, both candles give light. Similarly, when one mitzvah is performed, it drags another mitzvah, and both mitzvot are fulfilled. And this is the aspect of the ner tamid [everlasting light] that burns in the Temple, which is lit from the previous day's flame. And it is called "ner tamid" because it is always burning, for the mitzvot are continuous and never cease.

נֵר שֶׁof מִצְווֹת מִנֵּר שֶׁof מִצְוָה קוֹדֶמֶת

And this is also the aspect of Torah study, for one topic of Torah study drags another topic. For Torah is compared to fire, as it is written: "Is not My word like fire?" (Jeremiah 23:29). And like fire, it spreads and consumes. Similarly, Torah study spreads from topic to topic, and one topic drags another. And through this continuous study, one merits to have a candle of Torah that always burns. And this is the aspect of the menorah [candelabrum] in the Temple, which has seven candles, corresponding to the seven sefirot [divine emanations]. And the light of the menorah illuminates the entire Temple, for the light of Torah illuminates the entire world.

Therefore, a person should always be careful to perform mitzvot and study Torah continuously, for one mitzvah drags another, and one topic of Torah study drags another. And through this, he merits to have a continuous light of mitzvot and Torah that illuminates his soul and the entire world. And this is the aspect of the ner tamid that burns forever in the Temple, which represents the eternal light of Torah and mitzvot that should burn in the heart of every Jew forever.
