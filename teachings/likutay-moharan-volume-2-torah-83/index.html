"תּוֹרָה צְרִיכָה לִלְמוֹד קוֹדֶם לְמֶלֶךְ"

Torah must be studied before a king, for Torah is a portion of HaKadosh Baruch Hu [the Holy One, blessed is He]. And the king is also a portion of HaKadosh Baruch Hu. And through studying Torah before the king, the light is doubled. And this is: "From before Hashem he went" (Deuteronomy 17:3), that the Torah of the king is before Hashem, as it is written: "And you shall set a king upon you, etc." (Deuteronomy 17:15), and this is "Melech HaMelech" [King of kings], as it is written: "Who is this King of glory?" (Psalms 24:10). "The L-rd of hosts" [Hashem Tzvaos], the Lord of hosts is the king.

תּוֹרָה צְרִיכָה לִלְמוֹד קוֹדֶם לְמֶלֶךְ

And through studying Torah before the king, the light of the Torah is doubled, for it is doubled with the portion of the king, which is also from HaKadosh Baruch Hu. And "the light of Torah doubles" is the aspect of "And you shall call on the name of Hashem" (Genesis 4:26), which is also doubled. And this is the aspect of the two crowns that were given to Israel: the crown of Torah and the crown of kingship. And through the Torah before the king, both crowns are revealed, as our Sages, of blessed memory, said (Shemos Rabbah, Parashah 28): Why were the two crowns given? Because at the sea they sang, etc. And the aspect of the two crowns is "He will be great" (Genesis 26:13), etc.
