Likutay Moharan - Handwritten Manuscripts

"כִּי נֵר מִצְוָה וְתוֹרָה אוֹר"

← Back to Likutay Moharan Torah 116

These are the handwritten manuscripts of Rabbi Nachman of Breslov, of blessed memory, which have been preserved from his original writings. These manuscripts contain the authentic words of the tzadik [righteous one], written in his own hand, and they carry a special holiness and power. The manuscripts include various teachings, conversations, and notes that were written by Rabbi Nachman himself, and they have been carefully preserved by his students and followers throughout the generations.

כִּי נֵר מִצְוָה וְתוֹרָה אוֹר

The manuscripts are considered to be of extreme importance, as they represent the direct words of Rabbi Nachman without any interpretation or addition. They contain deep insights into the teachings of Breslov Chassidus, as well as practical advice for serving Hashem. The manuscripts also include conversations that Rabbi Nachman had with his students, where he revealed profound secrets of Torah.

The study of the manuscripts is considered a great merit, as it allows one to connect directly with the tzadik. Many Breslov Chassidim consider the study of the manuscripts to be essential for understanding the depth of Rabbi Nachman's teachings. The manuscripts have been published in various collections, and they continue to inspire and guide Jews around the world.

It is customary to recite the manuscripts with reverence and awe, recognizing that these are the actual words of the tzadik. The manuscripts are often studied in depth, with careful attention to every word and nuance. Through the study of the manuscripts, one can gain a deeper understanding of the path of teshuvah [repentance] and the service of Hashem as taught by Rabbi Nachman of Breslov.
