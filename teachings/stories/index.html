Wondrous Stories and Teachings of Rabbi Nachman of Breslov

Rabbi Nachman's stories are profound allegories filled with mystical secrets and life lessons. He taught that stories have the power to arouse people from their slumber and bring about transformation.

He said: "Telling over stories is propitious to arouse people from their slumber" - contrary to the common belief that stories induce sleep.

"Look, and discern, and gaze, you who study this, how far these matters reach. Fortunate is one who attends and will attain to know and grasp a little of the secrets of these stories."

Tale 1 - The Lost Princess

Seeking What Was Lost

A king accidentally banishes his daughter to a place "not good." A brave searcher endures years of trials - fasting, sleeping for decades, seeking the golden mountain with a pearl castle - to rescue her. The princess herself waits patiently, sustained by her faith that she would be saved. This tale speaks of the soul's journey back to its source and the power of determined faith.

Tale 2 - The King and the Emperor

A Tale of Love and Separation

An emperor and king promise to match their children. The prince and princess fall in love at school, are espoused with a ring, but through trials of pirates, storms, and mistaken identities, they are finally reunited. This story illustrates the power of true love and divine providence in bringing soulmates together.

Tale 3 - The One Who Could Not Walk

Healing Through Strange Herbs

A sage's son who cannot walk embarks on business, is robbed, eats grass, finds a magical diamond, and journeys to where day and night meet - hearing the sun and moon converse about salvation. This profound tale reveals how even the most desperate situations can lead to unexpected salvation through faith.

Tale 4 - The King Who Decreed Apostasy

The Power of Tallis and Tefillin

A king decrees conversion or exile. Through secret Jews, a wise minister, astrologers, and a mysterious effigy of seven metals, the story reveals how Jews dressed in tallis and tefillin can pass through fire. This tale demonstrates the power of Jewish faith and symbols in times of persecution.

Tale 5 - The King's Son Made of Precious Stones

The Promise Fulfilled

A childless king and queen receive a son made entirely of gemstones through a tzaddik's blessing. When the jealous princess casts a spell, he becomes leprous, but ultimately the prophecy is fulfilled. This story speaks of the purity of the tzaddik and the ultimate triumph of good over jealousy.

Tale 6 - The Humble King

A King Concealed in Humility

A wise man searches for a king who signs himself as "mighty, truthful, and humble." Through understanding the country's jesting and false courts, he discovers a king so humble he hides behind a veil. This parable teaches that true greatness lies in humility.

A King Seeks Understanding

At a banquet, a king watches a spider and fly protected by a book's page. This leads him to understand that Jews protect the world, and he travels to find a sage who reveals the mysteries of providence. This tale speaks of Israel's role in guarding the world.

The Deception of the Evil Inclination

A rabbi repeatedly prevents his son from visiting a tzaddik. After the son dies, he appears in dreams telling his father to go - revealing that the Satan first incited, then killed, then mocked the son. This story warns against the cunning tactics of the evil inclination.

Philosophy vs. Simplicity

Dreams of Exchange

Wealthy burgher and poor neighbor each dream of the other's fortune. When the pauper's wife is abducted, the burgher rescues her at great risk, and their children eventually unite a pauper-turned-emperor with extraordinary beauty. This speaks of divine providence and unexpected destiny.

The Exchanged Children

A midwife switches a king's infant with a servant's. The false prince becomes king and exiles the true heir. Through wilderness, strange men, and spiritual trials, the exiled prince seeks truth and redemption. This tale explores themes of identity and spiritual growth through adversity.

A Country That Believed Only in Money

Tale 13 - The Seven Beggars

The Complete Remedy

Each of seven beggars has a unique deficiency - one lacks eyes, another legs, etc. Together they represent the wholeness that comes from all parts of Israel uniting in prayer and faith. This is the culmination of all stories, teaching that tikkun comes through unity.
