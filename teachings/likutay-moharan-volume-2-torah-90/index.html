Likutay Moharan Volume II - Torah 90

"עֶשֶׂר לְשׁוֹנוֹת שֶׁל שִׁיר בַּתּוֹרָה"

← Back to Likutay Moharan Torah 87 Torah 93 →

There are ten expressions for song [shirah] in the Torah, as our Sages, of blessed memory, said (Sotah 30b): Ten expressions for song were said at the Sea, as it is written: "Then sang Moshe and the children of Yisrael" (Exodus 15:1), "And they said, saying" (Exodus 15:21), "And it was when the singers" (Numbers 21:17), etc. And these ten expressions correspond to the ten sefirot [divine emanations]. And every song contains all the ten sefirot, but the ten expressions are subdivided into the aspects of the soul: Nefesh, Ruach, Neshamah, Chayah, Yechidah. The five levels of the soul are called "shir" [song] five times, and the ten sefirot are the ten expressions. And all this is in the song.

עֶשֶׂר לְשׁוֹנוֹת שֶׁל שִׁיר בַּתּוֹרָה

And through song, the entire soul is elevated and purified. For song has the power to elevate and connect the soul to its source. And this is the aspect of: "Sing to Hashem, for He has been highly exalted" (Exodus 15:21), as our Sages, of blessed memory, said: "The Sea saw and fled" (Psalms 114:3), and so forth. For through the song, the sea was split, which is the aspect of the lowering of the heights and the raising of the depths. And through this, the entire creation was elevated. And this is the power of song. And it is also written: "Serve Hashem with joy, come before Him with exultation" (Psalms 100:2). For joy is essential for serving Hashem. And through joy, one can serve Hashem properly.
